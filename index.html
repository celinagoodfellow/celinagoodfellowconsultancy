The Problem

The problem I keep seeing.

When I worked in Capital Markets at Lloyds Bank Corporate Markets in London, I watched something happen repeatedly.

Brilliant professionals — people who knew their numbers inside out, whose analysis was sharp and whose judgement was sound — would lose the room.

Not in obvious ways. Not through ignorance or nerves. They lost it in the gap between what they knew and how they delivered it.

An investor call where the message softened under scrutiny. A board meeting where a strong recommendation failed to land. A cross-border presentation where clarity dissolved under pressure.

The expertise was there. The communication didn't hold up.

That gap is what I work on.

About Celina

I work from both sides of the table.

I spent five years inside Capital Markets at Lloyds Bank Corporate Markets, coordinating international investor roadshows and working alongside senior finance professionals across global markets. I saw first-hand how financial communication works under real pressure — the language of investor scrutiny, the structure of high-stakes decisions, the difference between a presentation that moves a room and one that doesn't.

After Lloyds, I spent over a decade in education, training as a teacher and working in some of London's most demanding classrooms. That experience gave me something most communication coaches don't have — a deep understanding of how people actually learn, change their habits, and build new skills under pressure.

I bring both worlds into my coaching. Finance gives me the context. Teaching gives me the method.

My clients don't just communicate better. They build a voice that earns attention and holds a room.

Communication isn't a skill to polish. It's a pattern to change.

Most communication training focuses on techniques — what to say, how to structure a presentation, tips for speaking clearly. That's not what I do.

I work on the underlying patterns that determine how you show up under pressure. The habits that cause over-explanation. The instincts that soften a message at the moment it needs to be direct. The gap between how you think and how the room receives you.

That's why the results are lasting — not just for the next presentation, but for every high-stakes moment that follows.
